🧠 Part A – Case Study (Phishing Attack)

* *Title* : Phishing Attack on a College Student

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details by pretending to be a trusted entity. This usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites.

In a typical scenario, a college student receives an email that appears to be from their bank or a popular payment app. The message claims that their account will be blocked unless they verify their details immediately. It contains a link that looks genuine but actually leads to a fake website. When the student enters their login credentials and OTP, the attacker captures this information and gains access to their account.

Students and young users are common targets because they frequently use digital platforms like UPI apps, email, and social media but may not always verify authenticity. Attackers take advantage of urgency and fear to make victims act quickly without thinking.

The consequences of phishing can be serious. Victims may lose money from their bank accounts, have their personal data stolen, or even face identity theft. In some cases, compromised accounts are further used to scam others.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness, careful verification of links, and avoiding sharing sensitive information online.
